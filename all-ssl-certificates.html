Frequently Asked Questions:

An SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) certificate is a digital certificate that encrypts the data transmitted between a user’s browser and your website. This ensures that sensitive information—like login details, credit card numbers, and personal data—is kept secure and private.
You need an SSL certificate for your website because:
  • Security: It protects your visitors’ data from hackers and cyber threats.
  • Trust:It shows a padlock icon in the browser, assuring users your site is safe.
  • SEO Boost:Search engines like Google prioritize secure (HTTPS) websites in rankings.
  • Compliance:It helps meet privacy and data protection standards, especially for eCommerce and login-based sites.

There are three main types of SSL certificates, each offering different levels of validation and trust:
  1. Domain Validated (DV) SSL Certificates: These provide basic encryption and are the quickest to issue. Validation only requires proving ownership of the domain—ideal for personal websites or blogs.
  2. Organization Validated (OV) SSL Certificates:These offer a higher level of validation. In addition to domain ownership, the certificate authority (CA) verifies the organization’s identity. Suitable for small to medium businesses.
  3. Extended Validation (EV) SSL Certificates:These provide the highest level of trust and validation. The CA conducts a thorough vetting process to confirm the organization’s legal and physical existence. EV SSL certificates display the organization’s name in the browser’s address bar, making them ideal for e-commerce sites and financial institutions.
Additionally, SSL certificates can be categorized by the number of domains they secure:
  • Single Domain: Secures one domain only.
  • Wildcard: Secures one domain and unlimited subdomains.
  • Multi-Domain (SAN): Secures multiple domains with one certificate.

For most small business websites, a Domain Validated (DV) SSL certificate is the best choice. It offers quick issuance, basic encryption, and is cost-effective—perfect for securing login pages, contact forms, and general browsing. If your business handles customer data, payments, or wants to build stronger trust, consider upgrading to an Organization Validated (OV) certificate, which displays verified business details and adds more credibility.

Yes, our Multi-Domain (SAN) SSL certificates secure several domains under one certificate.

Absolutely! Our Wildcard SSL certificates cover a domain and all its subdomains (e.g., *.yourdomain.com).

SSL certificates are used to secure the connection between a user's browser and a website. They encrypt the data exchanged, protecting sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, and personal details from being intercepted by hackers. SSL certificates also help build trust with users by displaying the padlock icon and enabling HTTPS in the browser, showing that the site is safe and authentic.

Yes, SSL certificates can be used on multiple servers, but it depends on the type of certificate and your server configuration. Standard SSL certificates can be installed on more than one server, as long as you export and import the certificate along with its private key correctly. This is common in load-balanced or clustered environments. However, some providers may have licensing restrictions, so it's important to check their terms. For easier multi-server deployment, a wildcard or multi-domain (SAN) SSL certificate may be more suitable.
